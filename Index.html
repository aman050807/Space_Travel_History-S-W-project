The Space Exploration Timeline

1957

Sputnik 1

The world's first artificial satellite launched by the Soviet Union, marking the beginning of the space age.

Sputnik was only about the size of a beach ball but started the space race

1961

First Human in Space

Yuri Gagarin becomes the first human to journey into outer space, completing one orbit of Earth.

Gagarin's flight lasted 108 minutes and he became an international celebrity

1969

Apollo 11 Moon Landing

Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin become the first humans to walk on the Moon during NASA's Apollo 11 mission.

Armstrong's famous words: "That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind"

1971

First Space Station

Salyut 1, the world's first space station, is launched by the Soviet Union.

Salyut 1 was only 20 meters long and weighed 18,900 kilograms

1981

First Space Shuttle

Columbia becomes the first Space Shuttle to reach orbit, beginning the era of reusable spacecraft.

The Space Shuttle could carry up to 8 astronauts and 50,000 pounds of cargo

1998

International Space Station

The first module of the ISS is launched, beginning construction of the largest structure in space.

The ISS is the size of a football field and orbits Earth every 90 minutes

Space Exploration Milestones

First Satellite

Sputnik 1 launched on October 4, 1957

Secret Fact

Sputnik 1 was about the size of a beach ball and transmitted radio signals for 21 days before its batteries died.

First Animal in Space

Laika the dog orbited Earth in 1957

Secret Fact

Laika was a stray dog from Moscow. Her flight proved that living organisms could survive space travel.

First Spacewalk

Alexei Leonov in 1965

Secret Fact

Leonov's spacesuit inflated in the vacuum of space, making it difficult to re-enter the spacecraft.

First Moon Landing

Apollo 11 in 1969

Secret Fact

The lunar module had only 20 seconds of fuel remaining when it finally touched down on the Moon's surface.

First Space Station

Salyut 1 in 1971

Secret Fact

The first crew to board Salyut 1 died during re-entry when their Soyuz capsule depressurized.

First Mars Rover

Sojourner in 1997

Secret Fact

Sojourner was only designed to operate for 7 days but continued working for 83 days on Mars.
